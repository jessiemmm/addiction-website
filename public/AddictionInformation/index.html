Social Media Statistics

Social media addiction affects people of all different ages, as it has become part of the daily habits across multiple generations. In 2020, Americans spent an average of 2 hours and 25 minutes a day on social media, and this time has significant negative effects on their mental and physical health. Social media usage, according to a 2018 British study, leads to disrupted sleep, depression, and even memory loss. It can also lead to an increased sense of loneliness, according to a study from the University of Pennsylvania.

Social media has become so addictive because the action of disclosing oneself on these apps activates the same part of the brain that lights up when one uses an addictive substance. This “reward-area” in our brains can affect our sensations and decisions. Using an addictive substance and experiencing something rewarding activates the same neurons in our brain, raising our dopamine levels. Thus, our brains associate an activity, such as media usage or drug use, with a positive reward. The similarities between our neurological reactions to drugs and social media reveals the serious nature of this addiction.

Recovery from Social Media Addiction

It is important to reaffirm aspects of your life that were neglected by your use of social media. You can spend time with your family outside of your screen and pick up new hobbies that don’t involve your phone or the internet.

No matter what, you need to know that it is never too late to get control of your addiction. Recovery from social media addiction could be the most important thing you do in your life, and could help generations after you.
